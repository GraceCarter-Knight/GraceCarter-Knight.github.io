The golden lion tamarin is a small monkey native to the eastern rainforests of brazil and is an edangered species.

GLTs share their food with others in the group, which is called a troop their groups range from 2-8 members.

Their scientific name is leontopithecus rosalia.

When GLTs spot a pretator, it will give an alarm call.

Golden lion tamarins is so named because of the reddish orange mane that surrounds its dark, hairless face.
